## Core Insight

Perplexity's breakthrough isn't better search — it's eliminating the "ten blue links" paradigm entirely. By providing synthesized answers with inline citations, they transformed search from "finding pages" to "getting answers."

## My Analysis

What makes Perplexity interesting from a product lens:

- **Citation-first design** — every claim is backed by a numbered source. This builds trust in AI-generated content in a way that ChatGPT's approach doesn't.
- **Follow-up questions** — the suggested follow-ups create a research workflow, not just a single query. It turns search into exploration.
- **Pro Search** — the step-by-step reasoning display builds user confidence and creates a "working with you" feeling.

The risk: they're competing with Google's distribution advantage using a fundamentally better product. History says distribution usually wins, but the product gap here is unusually large.
